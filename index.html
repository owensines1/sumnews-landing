Why SumNews exists

The feed is broken.
Stories aren't.

Modern news consumption is fragmented by design. Algorithms reward engagement, not understanding. You see dozens of headlines about the same story, each framed differently, with no clear picture of what's actually true.

SumNews is built on the belief that the right unit of news isn't a headline — it's a story. When you understand the story, you understand the news.

We focus on breadth, neutrality, and structure: showing you how widely a story is covered, presenting the facts without spin, and making it easy to go deeper when you want to.

Central banks in Europe and the US signal diverging paths on interest rate policy

Neutral Summary

The European Central Bank has signaled it may lower rates within the next two quarters amid slowing growth in the eurozone, while Federal Reserve officials have indicated they intend to hold rates steady until inflation falls closer to the 2% target. The divergence reflects differing economic conditions on each side of the Atlantic and is expected to influence currency markets in the near term.

Key Facts
  • ECB president confirmed rate cuts are "actively under discussion" at the next policy meeting.
  • US CPI came in at 3.4% for the most recent reporting month, above the Fed's target.
  • The euro fell 0.6% against the dollar following the ECB statement.
  • Analysts at three major banks revised their eurozone growth forecasts downward.
